01Overview

The context

Enterprise tools are dense, high-stakes, and used all day by expert operators. Small friction compounds into real operational cost. The brief: bring clarity and consistency to a platform that had grown feature-first.

I owned the experience end-to-end — from framing the problem with research, through interaction and visual design, to working with engineering on delivery.

A good enterprise tool should feel obvious — even when the work behind it isn't.
02Problem

What wasn't working

  • Inconsistent patterns — the same action looked and behaved differently across modules.
  • Cognitive overload — every screen exposed everything, all at once.
  • Slow task completion — power users built workarounds to move faster.
Before-state screenshot / audit · 16:8
03Process

How I approached it

I paired qualitative research with structured analysis — interviewing operators, mapping their real workflows, and pressure-testing assumptions against evidence rather than opinion.

Research / journey map
Wireframes / flows

From there, I defined the interaction principles and a component direction that the team could scale — prioritising the few jobs that mattered most.

04Solution

The design

A calmer, more consistent system: a clear hierarchy, progressive disclosure for complexity, and patterns that behave the same everywhere. Less to learn, faster to act.
